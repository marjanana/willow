<p><span style="font-size:20px;"><span style="font-family: helvetica,helvetica neue,arial,sans-serif;">Besides tapping into the message of the stone, the physical work itself looks so challenging. Mildred uses a hammer and chisel, a saw to chip through the stone, and sandpaper to produce the incredibly soft and supple texture of her finished pieces. She wears heavy gloves, goggles, a mask, and work clothes. As I photographed her, I could see why, as little chips of stone hit me nonstop and stone dust filled the air. The dust was ingrained in her work clothes, gloves and hair and mixed with the sweat on her face; it reminded me of the volcanic dust I experienced in Yogyakarta, Indonesia after Merapi&#39;s volcanic eruptions. Needless to say, after my visit to Mildred&#39;s studio,&nbsp; I gave my camera a good dusting off.</span></span></p>
