	<br/>
	I also create computer music and write lyrics to it. I do all my own recordings in my home.<br/>

	I never try to push for an idea unless I'm making something on deadline. Otherwise my ideas come to me rather easily, just naturally I guess. It’s all “play” for me. I always want to create something engaging, fun and new and luckily I can tap into that pretty much whenever I want to.<br/>

	<span style="color:#ff0000;">How do you decide if an idea is viable? Do you act on all your creative ideas?</span><br/>
	As I mentioned before, these days most of my drawings are geared towards stories I am developing. So within the constraints of the stories not all of my ideas see their way to a finished product but it’s the whole process of editing out the good and bad that keeps it all going. Which is to say even the bad ideas are beneficial because they eventually lead me to the good ones.<br/>

	<span style="color:#ff0000;">What challenges have you faced in your creative work?</span><br/>
	The answer to this question is related to the previous question. I want to be able to make a comfortable living as an artist. It’s also not the easiest thing to be true to your own artistic ambitions without becoming a “sellout” to the corporate industries like Disney and Pixar. At the same time, these jobs can be rewarding but I think true creative freedom might be limited.<br/>

	Bio:<br/>
	Landon Shanks was born and raised in the suburbs of Houston, Texas. He's never had any formal education in art but he has innate drawing skills and has always had an immense interest in it. Over time he developed his skills through study of his craft.<br/>
	<br/>
	As a child he loved to watch cartoons such as Transformers, He-Man and Robotech and was highly entertained by Marvel comics and video games. This has not changed.<br/>
	<br/>
	Landon was first exposed to Japanese culture through playing the game Street Fighter 2. Later, when Landon spent 2 years in Japan he became interested in anime, Japanime, and Japanese video games; he finds Japanese art refined and eye-catching. All these things have helped to shape Landon's storytelling style. He doubts he would have been interested in art had he never watched all those cartoons, played all those video games, or read all those comics. Landon is forever grateful for being exposed to those things because the child inside him lives on and it just might be the reason he can create with such ease.</span></span></p>
